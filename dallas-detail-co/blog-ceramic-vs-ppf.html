Ceramic coating vs. PPF: which do you need?

They sound similar but do opposite jobs. Ceramic = gloss + easy cleaning. PPF = physical rock-chip armor. Here's how to choose.

Ceramic coating, in one line

A liquid nano-ceramic that bonds to your clear coat and cures hard and slick. It delivers a deep mirror gloss, makes water bead and sheet off, and resists UV, bird droppings, and bug acids — for 2–5 years. What it does not do is stop rock chips.

PPF, in one line

Paint protection film ("clear bra") is a thick, self-healing urethane layer applied over the paint. It physically absorbs rock chips, road debris, and scratches, and light marks self-heal with heat. It lasts 7–10+ years with a manufacturer warranty.

Which should you choose?

  • Daily driver, lots of highway? Front-end PPF first — chips are your real risk.
  • Want gloss + effortless washing? Ceramic over the whole car.
  • New or special car? Do both — PPF on impact zones, ceramic everywhere (it can go on top of PPF).

The bottom line: ceramic and PPF aren't competitors — they're a team. See our ceramic coating and PPF pages, or get a tailored recommendation.
