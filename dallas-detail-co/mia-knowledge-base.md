## 3. Services — deep detail

### Maintenance / Express Shine — from $129 (~90 min)
Hand foam wash, wheels/tires + dressing, interior vacuum & wipe-down, glass, spray sealant. For cars
already in good shape that need an upkeep refresh. Recommended every 2–4 weeks for daily drivers, or
monthly as coating maintenance.

### Full / Signature Detail — from $189 / $289 (3–5 hrs)
The complete reset: everything in Express **plus** clay-bar decontamination, iron/fallout removal,
a 1-step gloss enhancement (light polish), steam-cleaned interior, leather conditioning, and a
6-month sealant. Best for: first-time clients, seasonal deep clean, pre-sale/trade-in prep.

### Interior Detail — from $149 (standalone or add-on)
Steam extraction of carpets/upholstery, stain treatment, **odor removal**, **pet-hair removal**,
UV-safe dressing on plastics/vinyl, leather clean + condition, antimicrobial sanitization, glass.
Handles: coffee/food stains, pet hair embedded in fabric, smoke/musty odor, sticky residue, sand/salt.
Heavy pet hair, biohazard, or severe odor may carry an add-on fee — confirmed at drop-off.

### Paint Correction — from $399 (1–3 stage)
Machine polishing that permanently removes swirl marks, light scratches, water spots, oxidation, and
holograms to restore true gloss and clarity.
- **1-step (enhancement):** removes ~60–70% of defects; big jump in gloss; great for newer/lighter cars.
- **2–3 stage (multi-stage correction):** compounding + polishing + refining for heavily swirled or
  neglected paint; removes up to ~85–95% of defects.
- It is **paint removal** (leveling microns of clear coat), so it's done conservatively by trained techs
  with paint-depth readings. It does **not** fix deep scratches that have gone through the clear coat,
  dents, or chips. Correction is the proper prep step **before** any ceramic coating.

### Ceramic Coating — from $799 (2–5 year packages)
A liquid nano-ceramic (SiO2/Si-based) that chemically bonds to the clear coat and cures into a hard,
slick, hydrophobic layer.
- **Does:** deep mirror gloss, strong water beading/sheeting, far easier washing, UV/oxidation
  protection, resistance to bird droppings, bug acids, water spots, and light chemical staining.
- **Does NOT:** stop rock chips or deep scratches (that's PPF's job), and it's not "scratch-proof" —
  it's scratch-*resistant*.
- **Tiers:** typically 2-year, 3–4 year, and 5-year+ packages (more layers / pro-grade product = longer
  warranty). Wheels, glass, trim, and plastics can be coated too.
- **Prep is everything:** wash → decon → paint correction → panel wipe → coat. Coating locks in whatever
  is under it, so correction first matters.
- **Cure:** install takes 1–2 days; keep the car dry ~24 hours, avoid washing ~7 days. Comes with a
  written warranty + free maintenance guide; we offer annual inspection top-ups.

### Graphene vs ceramic (if asked)
Graphene coatings are a newer ceramic variant: slightly slicker, excellent water *sheeting*, better
resistance to water spotting, and less surface heat retention. Practical difference is modest; we
recommend based on the vehicle and budget.

### Paint Protection Film (PPF / "clear bra") — from $699 (partial → full body)
A thick, optically-clear, **self-healing** urethane film applied over the paint that physically
absorbs rock chips, road debris, and scratches.
- **Self-healing:** light swirls/marks in the film disappear with heat (sun or warm water).
- **Coverage options:** partial front (bumper, partial hood, mirrors), full front (full hood + fenders),
  track package, or **full-body**. High-impact areas (front end, rockers, A-pillars) are most common.
- **Warranty:** manufacturer-backed (e.g., 10-year against yellowing/cracking/peeling), registered in
  the owner's name.
- **PPF vs ceramic — they solve different problems.** PPF = physical impact protection. Ceramic = gloss,
  hydrophobics, easy cleaning, chemical/UV protection. **The premium setup is both:** PPF on the
  high-impact areas, ceramic over the whole car (ceramic can even go on top of PPF for slickness).

## 5. Aftercare & maintenance (what Mia coaches)
- **Ceramic, first week:** keep dry ~24 hrs; no washing ~7 days; avoid automatic/brush car washes ever.
- **Washing a coated car:** hand wash with pH-neutral soap, two-bucket method, soft microfiber, dry with
  a clean plush towel or blower. Touchless/foam is fine; **never** automatic brush tunnels.
- **Cadence:** wash every 1–2 weeks; a maintenance/decon detail 1–2× a year keeps coatings performing
  and warranty active.
- **Bird droppings, bug guts, tree sap:** rinse off ASAP even on a coated car — they're acidic.
- **PPF:** safe to wash after ~48 hrs; self-healing reactivates with sun/warm water.

## 7. Common scenarios → recommendation
- **Brand-new car:** best time for PPF + ceramic before daily wear/chips start. Add interior coating.
- **Leased car:** ceramic keeps it pristine for turn-in; interior protection avoids wear charges.
- **Used / neglected car:** Signature Detail + multi-stage correction, then coating to lock in the gloss.
- **Daily driver / Texas highways & sun:** full-front PPF for rock chips + ceramic for UV/heat & easy
  washing. Window tint helps interior heat (refer if not offered).
- **Pre-sale / trade-in:** Signature Detail + 1-step correction to maximize resale appearance.
- **EV / Tesla:** soft factory paint that swirls easily → gentle correction + ceramic; PPF on the front;
  we're experienced with Tesla panel/charge-port specifics and frunk/trunk seals.
- **Winter / road salt (DFW ice events):** wash undercarriage, maintain coating, PPF resists salt-spray
  pitting on the front end.
